Lasagna Bolognese

Description

This is Smitten Kitchen's Lasagna Bolegnese recipe. Described on their website as "my culinary Mount Everest", this lasagna will knock your socks off. It's a twenty layer monster of noodles, ragu, bechamel and cheese. Stemming from a hatred of traditional ricotta-included lasagna, this chef set off on a multi-year journey.

This is not a recipe for the time-constrained. Each ingredient is prepared from scratch by hand. The author recommends giving yourself and entire day to tackle the sauce. The next day is dedicated to noodles and layering. Be warned.

Ingredients

    Bolegnese Sauce

  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 large carrot
  • 2 ribs celery, coarsely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, coarsely chopped
  • 2 to 4 tbsp olive oil
  • Kosher Salt
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 pounds ground beef or 1lbs beef & 1lbs pork
  • 1.25 cups tomato paste
  • 2 cups red wine
  • Water as needed
  • 2 bay leaves
  • A few sprigs thyme, tied in a bundle

    • Pasta

  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 0.5 tsp sea or table salt, fine
  • 1 - 2 tbsp water, if needed

    • Bechamel Sauce

  • 0.5 cup unsalted butter
  • 0.5 cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups whole milk
  • 1 tsp fine sea or table salt
  • 1 clove minced garlic
  • Freshly grated nutmeg
  • Freshly ground black pepper

    • Assembly

  • 1 2/3 cups grated Parmesan cheese

Day 1: Make the Sauce

  1. Chop onion, caorrots, celery and garlic
  2. Head a Dutch oven over medium high heat. Once hot, coat the bottom of the pan with 2-3 tbsp of oil.
  3. Once oil is hot, add vegetables
  4. Season with salt and pepper
  5. Cook vegetables until they are evenly browned, stirring frequently
  6. Add beef and season with salt and pepper. Cook until browned, approx. 15 min.
  7. Add tomato paste, cook 3 - 4 minutes
  8. Add red wine and scrape anything that's been stuck to the pan.
  9. Reduce to 50%
  10. Add water to the pan until water is 1 inch above the meat
  11. Toss in bay leaves and bundle of thyme. Stir to combine
  12. For the next 3 hours, keep replacing the water, Add 1-2 cups at a time.

Day 2: Make the Pasta

\
  1. Combine all pasta ingredients in food processor. Run machine until ball is formed
  2. Place ball of dough on a lightly floured surface and invert a bowl over it. Let rest for 1 hour
  3. Dust working area with flour
  4. Working a quarter of the dough at a atime, run it through the pasta roller on the widest setting (usually 0).
  5. Repeat process, increasing pasta roller setting each time, until you reach the 2nd to last stop
  6. Lightly flour dough to prevent sticking
  7. Lay pasta on floured surface

Cook your Pasta

  1. Cut pasta into lasagna noodle shapes.
  2. Bring large pot of water to boil
  3. Prepare bowl of ice water
  4. Boil several squares of noodle at a time, for 1 - 2 minutes
  5. Remove pasta and place into bowl of icewater
  6. Repeat until all pasta is cooked

Make your Bechamel

  1. Melt butter in the bottom of a medium-to-large saucepan over medium heat
  2. Once melted, add in flour and stir. Cook for a minute, stirring constantly
  3. Start pouring in small amounts of the whole milk, whisking continuously
  4. Add salt, garlic, nutmeg and black pepper. Drop heat to low simmer
  5. Cook, stirring frequently, for 10 minutes

Assemble Lasagna

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees
  2. Place 1/4 cup bechamel sauce at the bottom of a 9x13 baking dish
  3. Add first layer of noodles, overlapping where necessary
  4. Ladle 1 cup of sauce over noodles, spreading evenly
  5. Drizzle 1/2 cup bechamel sauce
  6. Sprinkle 1/3 cup of parmesan cheese
  7. Repeat process 3 more times, then add one more layer of pasta
  8. Spread 1/4 cup bechamel over noodles
  9. Sprinkle the rest of parmesan over noodles
  10. bake for 30-45 minutes
